Lift Product History

  • Mid-2000s: Created by Wells Burke
  • Late 2000s: Sold to Verifone
  • Mid-2010s: Purchased by Circle K
  • ~2017: Wells Burke brought back into the fold for enhanced features

Core Problem: Printed signage can only do so much to advertise every possible promotion to customers

What Does Lift Do?

Problem Statement

Lift solves the problem of how to deliver a calendar of promotions to get customers into stores

Key Challenges

  • Average convenience store has 2,000 items
  • Top 100 items account for 90%+ volume
  • 50 different promotions in store at any time
  • 90% of baskets have at least 1 part of a possible promotion
  • Too much for cashiers to remember!

The Lift Solution

Result: Improves store sales by over 5%!

How Lift Works

Example Transaction

  1. Shopper walks up with a 20oz Coke
  2. Between transactions, customer display loops through ad slots
  3. When transaction starts, customer sees receipt display & loyalty keypad
  4. Lift suggests complementary items (e.g., chips)
  5. Shopper satisfaction surveys at the end
  6. Customers grab items on their way out after completing transaction

How Lift Integrates with PoS

Data Sources

  • Scanner: Lift reads input simultaneously with PoS
  • Virtual Journal (VJ): May or may not include UPC depending on PoS type
  • Receipt Printer: Older method
  • Pole Display: Shows transaction total

Key Concept: Treat Lift as another scanner using a scanner splitter

Scanner Splitter Technology

How It Works

Wells created a splitter that sends the scan gun signal to a second device (Lift)

Critical Limitation

All Lift can ever do is inject items into the PoS

Potential Problems

  • No method to prevent crosstalk (though highly unlikely)
  • Creates "item not found" entries in PoS (not recorded in VJ)
  • Very hard to troubleshoot!

System Architecture

Web Portal Components

  • Portal knows about: Items, Locations/Players, Creative, Settings
  • Core DB: MySQL
  • API Layer: "Snooze" extracts data source details
  • Data Warehouse: AWS Redshift
  • Search Cache: Elasticsearch

Data Flow
